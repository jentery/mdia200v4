Making Fanwork

"a creative work produced by one or more fans,
generally intended for other fans. . . . In fanworks, some element of a canon work — the source text or event — is taken and incorporated into a new
creative piece. The taken element can be the
characters, world setting, plot, stories, still images, video clips, or something else from the source." (thanks, Fanlore!)

Participatory Culture

What makes a fandom participatory?

Consider barriers, support, mentorship,
social connection + significance of contribution.

What makes a fandom toxic?

Consider anti-fans and anti-shippers.

Transformative Work

What makes a fanwork transformative?

Consider whether it's unofficial, non-commercial, signed/watermarked + following community rules.

How does the fanwork address, undermine,
support, or alter its source material?
