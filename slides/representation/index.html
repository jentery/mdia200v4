Representation + Media

Representation in media is a form of social control.

Media demonstrate power through exclusion.

Media reflect cultural power through stereotypes.

Media use genre to normalize stereotypes.

Audiences internalize stereotypes.

Audiences use appropriation,
counter-types, and deconstruction to
transcode stereotypes.

Here, I'm drawing from the work of Stuart Hall.

Personal + Social in Media

The interplay of personal and social identities
means our identifications with media --- our
experiences of feeling seen --- are
partial or contradictory.

I can, for instance, identify with a fantasy
character without assuming I am that character.

The act of transcoding a stereotype engages
such contradictions, while vulgar expressions of
power (such as stereotypes) aim to "flatten"
people's identities for social control.
